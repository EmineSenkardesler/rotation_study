A Bottom-Up Analysis Using Satellite Imagery and Markov Chain Modeling

17 Years of Data 1.4 Billion Pixels 8 States Analyzed 310M Acres

Data Processing Pipeline

  • Transition Extraction: Year-to-year crop changes computed for each pixel
  • County Aggregation: 71,299 county-year records with crop areas
  • Memory-Efficient: Chunked processing (5,000-row windows)
  • CRS: EPSG:5070 (Albers Equal Area)
  • NCCPI Integration: 200K pixels with soil productivity data

Methodological Framework

1
Markov Chain Analysis - Model crop transitions as stochastic processes
P(cropt | cropt-1) computed from pixel-level observations; higher-order chains (2nd/3rd) capture complex rotation cycles

2

↓

Key Findings & Expected Results

  • Dominant Pattern: Corn-Soybean rotation dominates (~71% of corn rotates annually)
    • Corn → Soybean: 63.2%
    • Soybean → Corn: 76.4%
  • Yield Benefit: +4.69 bu/acre overall; effect declining over time (7.22 early → -1.49 recent)
  • Insurance Benefit: -4.8% loss ratio for corn after soybean (p < 0.0001)
  • Temporal Trend: Rotation adoption increasing (+7.2% since 2008); continuous corn declining
  • Spatial Clusters: 4 distinct regions identified (Balanced C-S, Wheat-Mixed, Corn-Dominant, Strong Rotation)
  • Soil Interaction: Rotation benefits larger on marginal soils; high-NCCPI soils favor continuous corn
  • Risk Paradox: High-risk counties rotate less (r = -0.48); no adaptive response to losses
